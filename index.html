⬡ About Sam Mooibroek

Coaching from a player still at the top of his game

Sam Mooibroek (pronounced moy-brook) is one of Canada's most exciting young skips — a Brier competitor, three-time OUA champion, and top-10 ranked player on the national circuit. A member of the KW Granite Club, Sam participates at the highest levels of the sport.

Sam began competing at the club level — just like most players he coaches today. That foundation matters. He understands the club game from the inside: the tight draws, the pressure of a club championship final, the small adjustments that separate a good league player from a great one. He hasn't just passed through that level on the way up — he's used it to build the competitive instincts that now carry him to Briers and Grand Slams.

Sam went on to dominate university curling with Wilfrid Laurier, winning three straight OUA titles and a 2023 U Sports national championship, before reaching the national circuit with Grand Slam appearances, Canadian Pre-Trials, and the 2025 Brier.

That full-spectrum experience — from club ice to national championships — is exactly what makes Sam's coaching so effective. He knows what winning looks like at every level, and he'll build the specific skills, strategy, and mindset you need to get there.
